# product-thinking

> Before you build anything, you need to understand what's worth building and for whom.

Part of the [build-from-zero](https://github.com/ohanatais/build-from-zero) ecosystem.

Most product failures aren't technical. They happen because someone built the right thing for the wrong person, or the wrong thing for everyone, or a good thing in a market that was already saturated.

This repo covers everything that happens before the first line of code, the thinking layer that determines whether what you build will matter.

---

## What's inside

### Market research
How to research a market without a research team or a budget.

Where most people go wrong: they search for data that confirms their idea instead of data that challenges it. This guide teaches you to look for the signal that's actually there — not the one you want to find.

- How to map a market from scratch
- How to identify direct and indirect competitors
- How to read App Store reviews as user research
- How to use Reddit, forums, and communities as a research lab
- Red ocean vs. blue ocean — how to tell the difference before you commit

### Idea validation
How to know if an idea is worth building before you build it.

The goal isn't to prove your idea is good. It's to find the single most dangerous assumption underneath it — and test that first, cheaply, before you write a line of code.

- How to turn an idea into a set of hypotheses
- How to prioritize which hypothesis to test first
- Validation experiments that cost nothing
- How to interpret weak signals of demand
- What "validated" actually means (and what it doesn't)

### Personas
How to define who you're building for — in a way that actually informs decisions.

A persona is not a demographic profile. It's a specific person with a specific situation, specific frustrations, and specific reasons why existing solutions don't work for them. Generic personas generate generic products.

- How to build a persona from research, not from imagination
- The questions that reveal real motivations
- Jobs-to-be-done as a framework for understanding what people actually hire a product to do
- How to use your persona to make product decisions faster

### Positioning
How to define where your product stands — and why someone should choose it over everything else they could do instead.

Positioning isn't a tagline. It's a strategic decision about who you're for, what problem you solve, and what makes you the right answer for that specific person with that specific problem.

- How to map the competitive alternatives your persona actually considers
- The positioning statement format that forces clarity
- How to find the gap that's yours to own
- How positioning shapes copy, design, and product decisions downstream

### PRD
How to write a Product Requirements Document that guides a solo build.

The corporate PRD was designed for teams. A solo builder needs something leaner — clear enough to follow alone, specific enough to prevent scope creep, honest about what's in and what's out.

- What a PRD for a solo builder actually needs (and what it doesn't)
- How to prioritize features before you start building
- How to define "done" for an MVP
- The PRD template that works with Claude Code

---

## How to use this

Read the guides in order if you're starting from scratch. Each one feeds into the next — your market research informs your personas, your personas shape your positioning, your positioning drives your PRD.

If you're already mid-process, jump to wherever you are. But if something feels off downstream, the answer is almost always in a step you skipped upstream.

**Claude Code skills** for each stage live in [pm-skills](https://github.com/ohanatais/pm-skills). Install them in your project to use structured frameworks directly inside Claude Code.
