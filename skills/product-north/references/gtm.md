# gtm — Go-to-market

**Goal:** think about launch and adoption like a Product Director, not like someone who
just "announces it shipped." For a solo founder building in public, GTM is half the
game: positioning, narrative, channel, and how the launch turns into traction and
learning.

**Output:** a lean GTM plan (in the chat) — for whom, what the promise is, through which
channel, with what narrative, measured by which metric. It can become `decide` or feed a
copy/content step.

## Before you speak: study the real thing

Re-read the current positioning (a brand/positioning doc, or run `/positioning`), the
builder's content voice and rules if they have them, and what the feature actually
delivers. GTM on top of a promise the product doesn't keep is damage, not reach.

## The move

1. **Positioning before message.** Who it's for, what it competes against (even if it's
   "against doing nothing"), and the one thing it does better than the alternative. Use
   the positioning lens (April Dunford / Working Backwards — `references/lenses.md`).
   For the full canvas, this hands off cleanly to `/positioning`.
2. **The promise in one sentence.** The user value, in their language, no jargon. If you
   can't say in one sentence why they'd care, the problem is product, not GTM.
3. **Trigger and moment of value.** When/why does the user perceive the value (the aha
   moment)? GTM has to get the person to that moment as fast as possible — not just to
   sign-up.
4. **Channel and format.** Where the audience actually is, and which format serves the
   message. When it becomes real content, hand off to a copy/content step (e.g.
   `/product-copy`) — don't write the post here, recommend the angle.
5. **Honest build-in-public.** The behind-the-scenes / learning angle usually converts
   better than the announcement. What's the true story behind this feature? (No hype, no
   AI-jargon — match the builder's real voice.)
6. **Success metric + counterpoint (contract #1).** How will they know it worked (not
   vanity — reach ≠ adoption ≠ retention). And the steelman of "don't launch this yet."

## End

Recommend the concrete next step: `decide` (firm up the plan), a copy/content step
(produce the content), or holding the launch if `stress` shows the promise doesn't hold.
