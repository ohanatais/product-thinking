# lenses — Library of product frameworks and references

Load on demand. In any mode, **pick the right lens, name which one you're using, and use
its forcing question.** Don't dump the list; apply the 1–2 lenses that fit the case. The
rule: a lens exists to force a question you'd otherwise skip — use the question, not the
vocabulary. Each item below cites the work/author (the real product canon) and the
question it forces.

---

## 1. Find the right problem

- **Jobs-to-be-done** — *Competing Against Luck*, Clayton Christensen; Bob Moesta's
  "Switch" interview. Forcing question: *what progress is the person trying to make, and
  what do they "hire" today to do it?* Separate the functional, emotional, and social
  job. For many products the deepest job is emotional/social: to be seen, to not forget,
  to control one's own narrative.
- **Outcome-Driven Innovation** — Tony Ulwick, *Jobs to Be Done: Theory to Practice*.
  Forcing question: *what outcome does the user want to optimize (faster, fewer errors),
  and are we measuring it?* Operationalizes JTBD into desired-outcome metrics.
- **Continuous Discovery / Opportunity Solution Tree** — Teresa Torres, *Continuous
  Discovery Habits*. Forcing question: *does this solution connect to a real opportunity,
  or is it a solution in search of a problem?* Map outcome → opportunities → solutions,
  in that order.
- **Customer Development** — Steve Blank, *The Four Steps to the Epiphany*; Eric Ries,
  *The Lean Startup* (build-measure-learn, MVP). Forcing question: *what's the smallest
  experiment that tests the biggest uncertainty, outside the building?*
- **Assumption mapping.** Forcing question: *what has to be true for this to work, and
  which of those is untested faith?* High impact + low evidence = test it first.

## 2. Prioritize and decide

- **RICE / ICE.** Forcing question: *reach × impact × confidence ÷ effort — who wins
  when you're honest about the confidence?* Beware false precision.
- **Kano.** Forcing question: *is this basic (expected), performance (more is better), or
  delight?* Delight without the basics solved is waste.
- **Opportunity cost.** Forcing question: *doing this means not doing what?* Always ask.

## 3. Strategy and focus

- **Good Strategy / Bad Strategy** — Richard Rumelt. The kernel: diagnosis → guiding
  policy → coherent action. Forcing question: *what's the honest diagnosis of the
  problem, and are our actions coherent with each other — or is it a wish list disguised
  as strategy?*
- **Product discovery & empowered teams** — Marty Cagan, *INSPIRED* and *EMPOWERED*
  (SVPG). Forcing question: *are we solving a problem (outcome) or shipping a requested
  feature (output)?*
- **OKRs** — Christina Wodtke (*Radical Focus*), John Doerr (*Measure What Matters*).
  Forcing question: *what's the measurable key result, and are we over-focused on doing
  and under-focused on moving the needle?*

## 4. Judgment and biases (the devil's-advocate muscle)

- **Pre-mortem** — Gary Klein. Forcing question: *assume it failed in 3 months — tell me
  HOW.*
- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman. Forcing question: *what bias is pushing
  me here (anchoring, confirmation, overconfidence, sunk cost)?*
- **Product sense** — Shreyas Doshi, John Cutler. Forcing question: *am I optimizing the
  right problem, or just the one that's easy to measure / easy to build?*

## 5. Retention, activation, and growth

- **Aha moment / activation.** Forcing question: *what's the exact instant value is
  perceived, and how fast does the user get there?* Onboarding and GTM exist to shorten
  that path.
- **Hooked** — Nir Eyal: trigger → action → variable reward → investment. Forcing
  question: *what brings the person back without a push — what's the internal trigger
  (not the clock)?*
- **Growth loops & engagement** — Brian Balfour / Reforge; Andrew Chen, *The Cold Start
  Problem*. Forcing question: *is growth a self-reinforcing loop, or a funnel that
  leaks?* And: *does the product have the cold-start problem (little value alone early)?*
- **Retention curve & North Star** — Lenny Rachitsky (newsletter); the North Star
  playbook (Amplitude). Forcing question: *does the curve flatten (there's a core that
  stays) or go to zero (a forgettable vitamin)?* What's the one metric that captures
  value delivered, not vanity?
- **Hacking Growth** — Sean Ellis. Forcing question: *which growth experiment to run now,
  and what north metric does it move?*

## 6. Product-market fit and value

- **Sean Ellis PMF test.** Forcing question: *what % would be "very disappointed"
  without the product?* (~40% is the PMF signal.)
- **PMF** — Marc Andreessen ("the only thing that matters"); *Crossing the Chasm*,
  Geoffrey Moore. Forcing question: *do we already have a niche that loves it, or are we
  trying to please the mass market too early?*
- **Value metric & willingness to pay** — *Monetizing Innovation* (Madhavan Ramanujam).
  Forcing question: *would the user pay, and by what unit of value?* Reveals whether the
  value is real even without charging yet.
- **Escaping the Build Trap** — Melissa Perri. Forcing question: *do we measure features
  shipped or outcome for the user?* Output ≠ outcome.

## 7. UX and making the *user* awesome (not the product)

- **The Design of Everyday Things** — Don Norman. Forcing question: *is the affordance
  obvious, or are we blaming the user for a design error?*
- **Usability heuristics** — Jakob Nielsen (10 heuristics). Forcing question: *which
  basic heuristic does this violate (status visibility, user control, error prevention)?*
- **Badass: Making Users Awesome** — Kathy Sierra. Forcing question: *does this make the
  USER awesome at what they want to be, or just show off the product?*

## 8. Positioning and go-to-market

- **Obviously Awesome** — April Dunford. Forcing question: *what alternative does the
  user compare this to, and what's the one thing we do better in that context?*
- **Working Backwards / PR-FAQ** — Amazon (Bryar & Carr). Forcing question: *if I wrote
  the announcement and the FAQ BEFORE building, does the promise hold up?*
- **Value Proposition Design** — Osterwalder (also *Business Model Generation* / Lean
  Canvas, Ash Maurya). Forcing question: *does the value proposition explicitly match
  pain↔relief and desire↔gain?*

## 9. Scope and delivery (the 0→1 cut)

- **Lean Inception** — Paulo Caroli. Forcing question: *what's the MVP that delivers a
  whole thin slice of value, instead of half a feature?*
- **Product Backlog Building (PBB)** — Fábio Aguiar. Forcing question: *is the backbone
  of epics and features right before detailing stories?*
- **Software Product Management** — practitioner canon and product communities (e.g.
  PM3 / Cultura de Produto in Brazil). A base reference for product vocabulary and
  process, especially in the Brazilian context.

## 10. Bring your domain's canon

The lenses above are general product thinking. **Every domain also has its own canon** —
load it, because the deepest product decisions live where general product sense meets the
specifics of the thing you're building. Name the domain reference the same way you name a
lens.

As an *illustration*, here's a domain — **career and the talent market** — fully worked,
to show the shape of what "load your domain's canon" looks like:

- **Brag document** — Julia Evans, "Get your work recognized: write a brag document."
  Forcing question: *are we reducing the friction of recording an accomplishment at the
  moment it happens, before it's forgotten?*
- **Career capital** — Cal Newport, *So Good They Can't Ignore You*. Forcing question:
  *does this help the person accumulate and make VISIBLE their career capital (what they
  know and shipped), instead of just storing data?*
- **Career as a startup / permanent beta** — Reid Hoffman, *The Start-up of You*. Forcing
  question: *does the product treat a career as something actively managed over time, or
  as a static CV that only moves when it hurts?*
- **Impact evidence / promotion packet** — Will Larson (*An Elegant Puzzle*, *Staff
  Engineer's Path*). Forcing question: *does the data we capture become ammunition at the
  high-stakes moment (review, promotion, interview, negotiation)?*
- **Career as iterative design** — Bill Burnett & Dave Evans, *Designing Your Life*
  (Stanford). Forcing question: *do we help the person see patterns and directions in
  their own trajectory, or just list loose events?*

Whatever your domain, write down its 3–5 canonical references and the question each one
forces — that list is as important as the general lenses above.

---

## How to cite the lens

When you apply one, say in a line which and why: *"using JTBD (Christensen) here because
what matters is the emotional progress, not the feature"* — so the builder learns the
why, not just the conclusion. If you use two lenses that disagree, show the tension;
that's where the product decision lives.
