# Mode: Direct

For users who know what market research is, have done it before, and want to move fast.
No hand-holding. No explanations of why TAM exists. Execute the protocol, deliver the
output, surface the sharpest insights.

## Approach

Skip preamble. Read the idea from context. Restate in one line if needed, then go.
If something is ambiguous, make a reasonable assumption and name it — don't ask.
Surface tensions and gaps without softening them. The user can handle a direct read.

## Protocol

### Phase 1: Research plan (show before executing)

List 6–10 questions the research will answer. Show the plan before searching.
This forces clarity on what matters and gives the user a chance to redirect.

Examples:
1. Who are the 3–5 direct competitors? What's their positioning and pricing?
2. Who are the indirect competitors and what approach do they take?
3. What are the most common user complaints about existing solutions?
4. Are there products that died in this space? Why?
5. What's the realistic market size anchored to verifiable data?
6. Are there geographic or language-based gaps being ignored?
7. What does the status quo look like — what do people do without any tool?

### Phase 2: Execute searches

Run 6–10 searches. Prioritize:
- Competitor pricing pages (actual numbers, not claims)
- G2, Capterra, Product Hunt, App Store reviews (user behavior signals)
- Reddit, HN, Discord threads (real complaints and workarounds)
- Dead products in the space (what failed and why)
- LinkedIn user data, industry reports, or proxy markets for sizing

Apply Mom Test to everything: discard generic praise, extract behavioral evidence.

### Phase 3: Competitive map

| Player | Type | Approach | Pricing | Traction signals | Gaps | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Types: Direct / Indirect / Adjacent / Status quo

For each gap: is it a deliberate choice, a technical limitation, or a market opportunity?

### Phase 4: TAM/SAM/SOM

Build from real anchors. Show the calculation. Name every assumption.

Format:
```
TAM: [number] — [how derived] — [confidence: Confirmed/Estimated/Inference]
SAM: [number] — [derivation chain with named assumptions]
SOM: [number] — [grounded in user's actual reach capacity, not aspirational share]

Critical assumption: [the one assumption that, if wrong, changes everything]
```

No invented numbers. No round figures without source. If a number isn't findable,
say so and explain what data would be needed.

### Phase 5: Red/blue ocean read

Direct call. Not a hedge.

- What's crowded and why
- Where the white space actually is
- The most defensible wedge given what exists
- Whether the gap is a market opportunity or a graveyard (things fail for reasons)

### Phase 6: Strategic implication + next step

One tight paragraph. What does this mean for positioning, entry strategy, and
the riskiest assumption to test first?

Close with: `/validate-idea` (if the territory looks viable and the idea needs testing)
or a specific redirect if the research revealed a more fundamental issue.

```

### Market size
TAM: ...
SAM: ...
SOM: ...

Critical assumption: ...

### Red or blue ocean?
[Direct call with named wedge]

### Strategic implication
[One tight paragraph + next step]
```

## Anti-patterns (direct mode)

- Don't explain what TAM means
- Don't soften a red ocean read if the evidence points there
- Don't pad with "interesting findings" summaries — lead with the sharpest insight
- Don't do more than 10 searches without flagging it
- Don't omit the critical assumption — it's the most useful thing in the output
