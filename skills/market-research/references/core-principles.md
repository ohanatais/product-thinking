# Core Principles

Loaded by all modes. These govern how market research is conducted regardless of depth.

## On data quality

**Prefer behavioral signals over stated preferences.**
What people do > what people say they'd do.
- Strong signal: "I pay $20/month for X because it solves Y"
- Weak signal: "I'd love an app that does Z"
- Useless: "great product!" / "amazing tool"

**Source hierarchy (strongest to weakest):**
1. Direct behavioral data: pricing pages, App Store/Play Store downloads, review volume
2. Community evidence: Reddit threads, forum complaints, Discord discussions
3. Analyst reports with methodology disclosed
4. Press coverage (treat as marketing unless primary sources cited)
5. Founder/company claims (almost always optimistic — discount heavily)

**For TAM/SAM/SOM:**
- Always provide a range, never a point estimate
- Label each number: [Confirmed source], [Estimated], or [Inference]
- SOM must be grounded in the user's actual reach capacity — not aspirational market share
- If a number isn't findable with confidence, say "not available with confidence" and
  explain what would be needed to find it

## On competitor analysis

**Map four types, not just direct competitors:**

| Type | Definition | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Direct | Same problem, same audience, same approach | The obvious threat |
| Indirect | Same problem, different approach | Often the real competition |
| Adjacent | Different problem, same audience — could pivot in | The future threat |
| Status quo | What people do today without any tool | Often the hardest to displace |

**For each relevant competitor (3–7 total):**
- What they actually do (not their marketing)
- Pricing (from pricing page, not claims)
- Positive signals (traction evidence, not praise)
- Negative signals (user complaints, churn indicators, what they don't do)
- Source for each claim

## On market sizing

**Work backwards from behavior, not forwards from population.**

Bad: "There are 1 billion smartphone users, so if we capture 1%..."
Good: "There are 83M LinkedIn users in Brazil. ~15-25% are actively engaged with career
development. Of those, ~20-30% might pay for a tool. That's 2.5–6M potential users,
of which conversion to paid is historically 3-6%. So 75K–360K paying customers over
time — at R$240–480/year, that's R$18M–170M SAM."

The second version is still an estimate. But it's built from named assumptions that
can be tested and updated — not from population fantasy.

**Always name the assumptions.** The most important thing about a market size estimate
is not the number — it's which assumptions, if wrong, would make the number collapse.

## On red vs. blue ocean

**Red ocean signals:**
- Multiple well-funded competitors with overlapping positioning
- "Me too" products with marginal differentiation
- Price competition as primary dynamic
- High CAC, low organic growth in the space

**Blue ocean signals:**
- Workarounds in widespread use (spreadsheets, manual processes, Notion templates)
- Underserved segments with money and a real problem
- Positioning gap that incumbents can't fill due to their own constraints
- Geographic or language-based gap (strong signal when incumbents are all English-only)

**The honest read:**
Most products are neither pure red nor pure blue. The useful question is:
"Is there a specific wedge — a narrow enough entry point — where this product would
have a clear advantage over everything that exists?"

## On inference discipline

Structure all analytical sections as:

```
**Fact:** [claim] — [source or confidence marker]
**Inference:** [what this suggests] — [marked as my reading]
```

Confidence markers:
- [Confirmed] — sourced, verifiable
- [Estimated] — reasonable calculation from real anchors
- [Inference] — logical but not sourced — treat as hypothesis

## On output economy

Tables over prose for comparable data.
Point-first writing — the sharpest insight leads every section.
No "as we can see above" or "in conclusion" filler.
No invented numbers dressed as research.
If something is unknown, name it as unknown and note what it would take to find it.
