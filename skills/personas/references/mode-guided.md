# Mode: Guided

For first-timers. You've never built personas before, or you're not sure what
they're supposed to do. This mode walks you through the thinking — not as a tutorial,
but as a thinking partner who explains *why* each piece matters before asking for it.

## Tone

Same as market-research guided mode: senior PM who's done this a hundred times.
Direct. No jargon without definition. Not condescending.

## Before starting: ground the problem

Restate the product idea in one sentence and confirm before building personas.

> "Before we define who you're building for, let me make sure I understand the product:
> [restatement]. Is that right?"

Then explain what the session will produce:

> "We're going to define 1–2 personas — not profile cards with age and job title,
> but portraits of the *behavior* that drives someone to look for a product like yours.
> The most important thing we'll find is: what happens in their life that makes
> them start searching? That's the trigger moment, and it's the foundation of everything."

## Step 1: Who's already trying to solve this? (explain, then do)

**Explain:**
> "The first question isn't 'who is my user' — it's 'who's already dealing with
> this problem right now?' We're looking for people who have the pain, are aware
> of it, and are doing *something* about it — even if that something is a janky
> workaround. Those people are your early adopters."

**Then ask:** What do you know about who's struggling with this? Any communities,
forums, social media groups, or people you've talked to?

If they have some signal: pivot to behavioral mode.
If they have none: proceed with hypothesis mode questions below.

## Step 2: The trigger moment (explain, then ask)

**Explain:**
> "A trigger moment is the specific situation — not a feeling, but an event — that
> makes someone go from 'this is annoying' to 'I need to find a solution now.'
> Without a trigger, you don't know *when* your user is ready to be reached or
> what pushes them to actually change their behavior."

**Example:**
> "For a note-taking product, the trigger might not be 'wanting to be more organized.'
> It might be 'losing an important client detail right before a meeting and feeling
> the panic of looking unprepared.' That specific moment is where the search begins."

**Then ask:** Can you describe a moment — a specific situation — where someone
would feel the pain your product solves most acutely?

## Step 3: The workaround (explain, then ask)

**Explain:**
> "What does this person do today, without your product? This is important because
> it tells you two things: how much the problem hurts (they're already doing extra
> work), and what you're competing against. You're not competing with the category
> leader — you're competing with whatever habit or hack they've built."

**Then ask:** What's the clunky, imperfect thing your future user does today to
handle this? Spreadsheet? WhatsApp group? Hiring someone? Doing it manually? Ignoring it?

## Step 4: Build the persona card together

Use the template below. Fill it collaboratively — the user provides content,
the skill identifies patterns and flags gaps.

After completing each element, explain what it reveals:

> "The job statement '[X]' tells us that this person isn't just looking for a feature —
> they're trying to make progress on [outcome]. That's what the product needs to
> promise."

## Output structure (guided mode)

```
## Persona: [Name — optional, functional label works too]

**Job statement:**
"When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."

**Trigger moment:**
[The specific event that starts the search. Not a feeling — a situation.]

**Current workaround:**
[What they do today without your product. The status quo.]

**Pains (behavioral):**
- [Specific frustration — not generic]
- [...]

**Gains sought:**
[What "hired" looks like. The measure of success.]

**Key assumptions to validate:**
- [Element you inferred without evidence — and how to test it]
- [...]
```

## Closing (guided mode)

After the persona card, explain the next step:

> "With a persona defined, the natural next step is positioning — figuring out
> where your product stands vs. everything this person might consider instead.
> That's what `/positioning` does.
>
> Or, if you want to validate that this persona is real before going further,
> the fastest way is 3–5 conversations with people who match this description.
> I can help you write the interview script."

## Anti-patterns in guided mode

- Don't fill the persona card from your own assumptions without flagging them
- Don't produce 4+ personas — pick 1–2 and explain why that's the right scope
- Don't skip the trigger moment — it's the most important element
- Don't use demographic filler without tying it to a behavioral explanation
- Don't end without a concrete next step
